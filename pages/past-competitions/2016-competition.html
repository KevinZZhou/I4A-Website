2016 Competition

We are excited to present the results for the Ideas for Action competition 2016. Ideas for Action, a joint project of the World Bank Group and the Carol and Lawrence Zicklin Center for Business Ethics Research at the Wharton School, invited students and young professionals from around the world to share their ideas for financing and implementing the UN’s Sustainable Development Goals.

Participation was overwhelming with more than 490 registered teams representing 1550 youths from 125 different countries. Each proposal went through an extensive and intricate evaluation process. After three independent review stages, a final panel consisting of senior World Bank, Wharton, and private sector executives decided on the final ranking. Winners were selected based on the exceptional clarity, significance, creativity, and feasibility of their ideas.

1st Place: DLVR

Nigeria

"The winning proposal is an innovative yet practical approach to a peer-to-peer service for local communities in Nigeria that seeks to tackle logistical challenges for low-income people in emerging market nations. It would create an online technology platform that connects transporters and senders, to enable the completion of both scheduled and on-demand deliveries."

2nd Place: OINCS

Uruguay

"The second-place team describes a mobile crowdsourcing platform that enables users to quickly report and share information about crimes and vehicular traffic in their cities. OINCS promotes a better city life experience by helping people travel throughout the city faster and more safely."

3rd Place: NaukriSearch

U.S.A.

"The third-place team presents a dual platform employment service aimed at increasing transparency in the slum labor market of Mumbai by improving access to employment information and opportunities. The system enables slum residents to receive accurate, up-to-date information concerning potential employment opportunities within their region."

Runner-ups

HerLIFE

U.S.A.

"The Her Life team proposed a solution for the life insurance market for Chinese working mothers (CWMs) who need to protect their children from unanticipated financial risks and to enhance their ability to pay for long-term insurance products."

Five Out of Five

Hong Kong

"Five out of Five recommends the issuance of green bonds aimed at financing air pollution reduction initiatives including, but not limited to, technological upgrades, reconstruction of industrial composition, and air purification strategies in Shanxi province."

Wylde International

Kenya

"Wylde International introduces an impact performance measurement and valuation tool (“Impact Units”) that will enable impact creators running Community Social Responsibility (CSR) initiatives to develop social interventions that demonstrate real and measurable impact."
