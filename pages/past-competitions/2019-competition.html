2019 Competition

In 2019, we received 3,100 applications from 142 countries and territories, and more than 7,000 registered teams. Thanks to everyone who applied!

Read on below for the list of winners, runners up, and honorable mentions for the 2019 Ideas for Action competition.

1st Place: CareNX

India

"The 2019 winning proposal...addresses the problem of high neonatal morbidity and mortality in much of [India]...This team developed two interconnected, indigenous, and portable smartphone- enabled devices — Fetosense and U-Act — to monitor uterine contractions for early identification of and intervention with preterm labor in parts of the world that have few doctors and no equipment."

2nd Place: Gather

United Kingdom, Madagascar

"The second-place proposal...addresses outbreaks of enteric and diarrheal disease by using geospatial data analysis to direct investments in sanitation to high-risk areas...It has launched its services in Madagascar, the country rated fourth worst in the world for sanitation provision. This proposal...suggests establishing sanitation data hubs and a global Sanitation Data Commission to complement and bolster its work."

3rd Place: WellPower

Kenya

"The third-place team...proposes a tech-enabled, vertically integrated clean water provider and distribution network to address the issue of inadequate access to clean water, which affects at least 350 million people in Sub- Saharan Africa and 19 million people in Kenya, the target country...it proposes installing and operating a solar- powered water filtration system in communities."

Runner-ups

Eco Panplas

Brazil

"This team has developed a decontamination and package-recycling technology that does not need water and creates no waste. The prototype has processed 8 million lubricating oil containers over the past two years, recovering and selling 400 tons of recycled plastic for new packaging and recovering 15,000 liters of oil for reuse."

Ekomuro H20+

Colombia

"Ekomuro H20+ has produced a rainwater harvesting technology made from reused polyethylene terephthalate (PET) bottles. The team aims to serve schools and homes by disseminating its idea to educational communities. It has already conducted outreach to 40 families in Comuna 4 and implemented the project in eight schools in Bogotá."

DamoGO

Indonesia/Republic of Korea

"DamoGO...tackles the problem of food waste through a mobile app that allows people to purchase unsold food before it is thrown away. The team partners with restaurants and food retailers so that users can search for and pick up discounted food...The DamoGO team has launched its platform in 15 stores in Seoul and has reported profitable turnovers for each store."

Komodo Water

Indonesia

"Komodo Water introduces a solution for improving access to clean water and fresh fish supplies from the brackish water sources in the Paparang Islands and Komodo National Park in Indonesia. Through reverse osmosis and ice block machines, powered by solar energy, fishing communities are able to purify brackish water into drinking water and create ice."
